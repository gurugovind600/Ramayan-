Bal Kand

Balkand is a part (kand or step) of Valmiki 's Ramayana and Goswami Tulsidas 's Shri Ram Charit Manas .
In the city of Ayodhya there was a king named Dasharatha who had wives named Kaushalya , Kaikeyi and Sumitra . Ayodhyapati Dasharatha performed Putrakameshti Yagya on the orders of his guru Shri Vashishtha to get children, which was performed by the sage Ringi. Agnidev was pleased to receive the devotional offerings and he himself appeared and gave the Havishyapatra (Kheer, Payas) to King Dasharatha, which he distributed among his three wives. As a result of consumption of kheer, Rama was born from Kaushalya's womb, Bharata from Kaikeyi's womb and Lakshmana and Shatrughna from Sumitra's womb.
When the princes grew up, sage Vishwamitra asked King Dasharatha to take Rama and Lakshmana with him to protect the ashram from the demons. Rama killed the demons like Tadka and Subahu and sent Maricha across the ocean by killing him with an arrow without fruit. On the other hand, Lakshmana killed the entire army of demons. On receiving the invitation of King Janak for the bow yagya, Vishwamitra along with Rama and Lakshmana came to his city of Mithila (Janakpur). On the way, Rama saved Gautam Muni's wife Ahalya. In Mithila, Sita, the daughter of King Janak, also known as Janaki, also organized a swayamvara, where, according to Janaki's promise, Rama married Sita by breaking the bow. Simultaneously with the marriage of Rama and Sita, Guru Vashistha got Bharata to Mandvi, Lakshmana to Urmila and Shatrughan to Shrutkirti.
